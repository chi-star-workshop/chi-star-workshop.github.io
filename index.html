Call for Papers

We are always reading, whether it is a research paper, news article, text message, chatbot response, or website. Better reading technology could transform texts on demand so that they are easier to read, surface hard-to-find information or support synthesis.

Recent changes in the technological landscape are significantly changing the reading experience. AI has introduced many new possibilities for interfaces to augment or transform text to be more rapidly scanned, navigated, understood, and compared to other texts. What does this future hold for reading?

Join us at the first CHI workshop on the Science & Technology of Augmented Reading (STAR). Come together with researchers from HCI and AI to work on exciting topics including but not limited to:

  • New designs for augmented reading, interfaces that in some way alter the text we read.
  • Reading interfaces in different domains, such as education, news consumption, literature synthesis, or reading for entertainment.
  • Contexts where reading is an integral but perhaps understudied task, such as legal reasoning, scientific discovery, or writing.
  • The future of reading with AI, or, does reading still matter?

Details

Accepted submissions will be posted in non-archival form on the workshop website and arXiv. One author of each accepted submission must attend the workshop and one day of CHI '26.

At the workshop, you will participate in round-the-room 1-minute lightning talks, followed by speculative design and research activities in small interest groups formed at the event. Groups will be enriched with brief primer documents on classic theories of reading. We can't wait to see you there!
